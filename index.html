Global Happiness Score 2023

How is the Global Happiness Score calculated?
The most widely known measure of global happiness is the World Happiness Report, which uses the Cantril ladder, also known as the "life satisfaction ladder," as a primary indicator. The key components commonly considered in calculating the global happiness score for a country include Life Satisfaction, GDP per Capita, Social Support Rate, Life Expectancy, Population and Freedom to Make Life Choices.
How useful is the Global Happiness Score?
Global happiness scores, such as those in the World Happiness Report, serve to guide policy decisions, enable international comparisons, assess public health, inform development efforts, and study the relationship between well-being and economics. They have implications for academic research, corporate social responsibility, and international diplomacy. Additionally, individuals use these scores to make decisions about where to live, work, or study, but it's important to remember the subjective nature of happiness and consider them alongside other indicators for a holistic well-being assessment.

Happiness Score affects Life Expectancy
As we can see, the higher the happiness score within a country, the higher the life expectancy of its people. Europe shows the highest happiness score and life expectancy, whereas Africa shows the least happiness score and life expectancy. Cuba takes the top spot in highest life expectancy of 78.89 years, and Mozambique is the last with lowest life expectancy of just 51.53 years.
