Happiness Report 2023

The World Happiness Report is the most widely-known indicatior, which uses the Cantril ladder, also known as the "life satisfaction ladder," as a primary indicator. The key components commonly considered in calculating the global happiness score for a country include Life Satisfaction, GDP per Capita, Social Support Rate, Life Expectancy, Population and Freedom to Make Life Choices.


Source: https://worldhappiness.report/

Happiness Score by Countries and Continents

The happiness score is the main indicator to assess how happy a country is. It is a score out of 10, with 1 represents the happiest and 0 represents the unhappiest.

Finland is the happiest country in the world, followed by Denmark.


However, Afghanistan is the unhappiest country in the world, followed by Lebanon.

Total Population by Countries

The number of population for each country has an effect on the happiness score of a country. For instance, a larger population can strain resources and potentially foster competition and inequality, leading to diminished happiness. Rapid population growth without corresponding economic development may also burden employment opportunities, thus impacting happiness score.


The Top 10 countries in the bar chart is visualized with China ranked at the highest position and most populated. This could be due to the social and economic conditions in the early years. However, other factors such as fertility rates, parental care, education and infrastructure could also influence population of countries around the world.

Happiness Score vs Life Expectancy

The happiness score of a country definitely affects its life expectancy of the country. This could be explained as higher levels of happiness within a society often correspond to better mental and physical health, leading to healthier lifestyle choices and reduced stress levels. Individuals in happier communities are more likely to adopt positive behaviors, such as regular exercise, balanced diets, and social engagement, which can contribute to improved overall health and longevity.


Europe shows the highest happiness score and life expectancy, whereas Africa shows the least happiness score and life expectancy. Cuba takes the top spot in highest life expectancy of 78.89 years, and Mozambique is the last with lowest life expectancy of just 51.53 years.

Average Social Support Rate of Developed and Developing Countries by Continents

The average social support rate which measures the perceived availability of help from family, friends, or other social networks, significantly influences the happiness score of a country as individuals feel more secure, valued, and understood within their communities with a plenty of support. Understanding and nurturing strong social support networks are crucial for fostering a happier and more resilient society.


It is a score out of 1, with 1.0 represents the most support received and 0.0 represents the no support received.

GDP per Capita (USD) by Countries and Continents

The relationship between a country's GDP per capita and its happiness score is nuanced. A higher GDP per capita generally correlates with increased access to material goods, better healthcare, and improved living standards, thus providing a happier environment to live in.
